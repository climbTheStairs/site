Letter dichotomies

About dichotomies

The easiest way to type using the Myer-Briggs Type Indicator is using dichotomies. Essentially, dichotomies are four sets of scales of preferences, shown by two different letters. These scales are:

  • Extraverted vs Introverted (E/I),
  • iNtuition vs Sensing (N/S),
  • Thinking vs Feeling (T/F), and
  • Judging vs Perceiving (J/P).

Each scale

Firstly, the E/I scale shows how a person takes in information. An extravert takes in information and makes decisions based on things all around them, while an introvert focuses on single things, often within them. Because of this, extraverts are typically—though not always—more interactive, social, and outgoing than introverts.

The second dichotomy is intuition and sensing. This is one of the more obscure properties that is often misunderstood. Simply stated, intuitives are curious and have a wandering mind that explores possibilities and visions of the future, while sensors are more grounded to this world, focusing on details and real things.

T and F stand for thinking and feeling, respectively. Thinkers approach situations and make decisions with logic and objectivity, while feelers tend to use emotion and morality. Statistics show that men are more likely to be thinkers, while women are more likely to be feelers.

The last, and probably most confusing scale is the judging/perceiving scale. Judgers are generally more organized, decisive, and authoritative; perceivers are less confident and orderly.
